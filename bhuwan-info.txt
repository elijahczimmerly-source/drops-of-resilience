Hi Bhuwan, looked at some other climate downscaling papers. Everyone that didn't use some kind of analog-based method used bilinear, and nobody questioned it except ISIMIP3, who considered conservative, and they didn't do any kind of quantitative testing. Their comparison was qualitative - they just assumed that providing smoother looking data as a starting point for their stochastic downscaling step would produce better outputs. So - no, nobody has used nearest neighbor for the regridding, but that's also not necessarily justified. I also looked into the analog-based approaches and generated some helpful visualizations to understand them, which are also attached. I concluded that some kind of deterministic regridding (bilinear interpolation or nearest neighbor) is definitely the right call for our pipeline.
