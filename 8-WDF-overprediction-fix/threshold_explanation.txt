What the threshold does

  During downscaling, DOR computes a precipitation value for each pixel-day as:

  y_final = y_base × noise_mult

  where y_base is the deterministic estimate (GCM × spatial_ratio) and noise_mult is stochastic spatial noise (~centered around 1.0).

  Not every day should be wet. During training, the pipeline looks at GridMET and measures what fraction of days are actually wet (≥ 0.1 mm) at each pixel in each semi-monthly period. If 40% of days are wet,
  it sets a threshold at the 60th percentile of the simulated y_base distribution — so that the bottom 60% of days get censored to zero. This calibration is done on the deterministic y_base values, without
  noise.

  At inference, the threshold is applied after noise is multiplied in. The PR_WDF_THRESHOLD_FACTOR scales the threshold up:

  if y_final <= threshold × PR_WDF_THRESHOLD_FACTOR:
      y_final = 0

  Why 1.15 was wrong

  The threshold is calibrated on the noise-free distribution, but applied to the noisy output. This creates an asymmetry:

  - A dry day with y_base just below the threshold can get noise-multiplied above it (e.g., 0.3 mm × 1.8 = 0.54 mm > threshold). That day becomes a false wet day.
  - A genuinely wet day with y_base well above the threshold almost never gets noise-multiplied below it (e.g., 5.0 mm would need noise_mult < 0.1, which is the hard clip floor).

  So noise pushes dry days wet much more easily than it pushes wet days dry. The net effect: too many wet days.

  Bhuwan recognized this and added the 1.15 factor — raise the threshold by 15% to compensate. But 15% wasn't enough. The baseline still overpredicted WDF by +3.4 percentage points (35.7% simulated vs 32.3%
  observed).

  How we got 1.65

  We swept PR_WDF_THRESHOLD_FACTOR over {1.15, 1.20, 1.25, etc} on the correct 216×192 Regridded_Iowa data, running the full pipeline for each value and comparing all metrics. The
  relationship is monotonic — higher factor = more aggressive censoring = fewer wet days. Factor 1.65 brings simulated WDF closest to observed.
